The Narkomfin Building is a block of flats at 25, Novinsky Boulevard, in the Central district of Moscow, Russia. Conceived as a "transitional type of experimental house", it is a renowned example of Constructivist architecture and avant-garde housing design, built in 1930 according to the project of architects Moisei Ginzburg 👷 and Ignaty Milinis 👨 for workers of People's Commissariat of Finance of the USSR (Narkomfin).

Though a listed "Cultural Heritage Monument" on the Russian cultural heritage register, it was in a deteriorating state for many years.
